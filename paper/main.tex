\begin{abstract}

We investigate a structurally constrained effective geometric sector exhibiting regular central behavior, Schwarzschild asymptotics, and admissible horizon interpolation.

Under static spherical symmetry, the horizon condition reduces to the cubic relation

\[
x^3-x^2+\beta^3=0,
\]

where

\[
x=\frac{r_H}{2M},
\qquad
\beta=\frac{r_c}{2M}.
\]

The resulting branch structure contains two admissible horizon branches separated by a critical merger point

\[
x_c=\frac{2}{3},
\qquad
\beta_c=\left(\frac{4}{27}\right)^{1/3}.
\]

The effective sector exhibits universal saddle-node scaling, modified photon-sphere structure, and finite curvature invariants.

The present work is deliberately restricted to the geometric reconstruction and consistency analysis of the TIG horizon sector within the stated effective framework.

\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

General Relativity successfully describes gravitational dynamics across a broad observational regime \cite{einstein1915, schwarzschild1916, wald1984, carroll2004}. Nevertheless, classical black-hole geometries contain singular structures where curvature invariants diverge and geometric predictability breaks down \cite{hawkingellis1973, penrose1965}.

Regularized black-hole constructions attempt to preserve asymptotic Schwarzschild behavior while modifying the deep interior geometry through effective structural scales or regularized sectors \cite{bardeen1968, dymnikova1992, ayonbeato1998, hayward2006}.

Topological Integrity Gravity (TIG) introduced a structurally constrained horizon sector governed by the cubic relation

\[
x^3-x^2+\beta^3=0,
\]

where

\[
x=\frac{r_H}{2M},
\qquad
\beta=\frac{r_c}{2M}.
\]

The purpose of the present work is not to establish a complete covariant theory of gravity. Instead, the goal is the geometric reconstruction and consistency analysis of the TIG horizon sector under a minimal set of admissibility assumptions.

The analysis is restricted to:

\begin{itemize}
    \item static spherical symmetry,
    \item Schwarzschild asymptotics,
    \item regular central behavior,
    \item one intrinsic correction scale,
    \item admissible horizon interpolation.
\end{itemize}

Within this framework, the horizon condition reduces to a universal cubic relation whose branch structure determines the admissible horizon sector of the geometry.

The present work therefore focuses on:
\begin{itemize}
    \item geometric reconstruction of the cubic sector,
    \item admissible branch structure,
    \item critical horizon geometry,
    \item photon-sphere behavior,
    \item curvature regularity of the effective sector.
\end{itemize}

\section{Effective Geometric Sector}

We consider the static spherically symmetric metric

\[
ds^2
=
-F(r)\,dt^2
+
\frac{dr^2}{F(r)}
+
r^2d\Omega^2.
\]

The effective lapse function is defined by

\[
F(r)
=
1
-
\frac{2M r^2}{r^3+r_c^3},
\]

where:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \(M\) denotes the asymptotic mass parameter,
    \item \(r_c\) defines the intrinsic correction scale.
\end{itemize}

The effective geometric sector satisfies:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Schwarzschild asymptotics,
    \item regular central structure,
    \item admissible horizon interpolation.
\end{itemize}

For large radius,

\[
r\gg r_c,
\]

the lapse approaches

\[
F(r)
\rightarrow
1-\frac{2M}{r},
\]

recovering the Schwarzschild limit.

For small radius,

\[
r\rightarrow0,
\]

the lapse behaves as

\[
F(r)
\approx
1
-
\frac{2M}{r_c^3}r^2,
\]

corresponding to an effective de Sitter-type near-core structure.

The resulting geometry therefore interpolates continuously between:
\begin{itemize}
    \item asymptotic Schwarzschild geometry,
    \item a regularized near-core sector.
\end{itemize}

The present work does not claim uniqueness of the effective geometric sector. The role of the effective geometry is restricted to the reconstruction of the TIG horizon structure.

\section{Horizon Reduction}

Horizons are determined by

\[
F(r_H)=0.
\]

Substituting the effective lapse function gives

\[
1
-
\frac{2M r_H^2}{r_H^3+r_c^3}
=
0.
\]

Rearranging yields

\[
r_H^3+r_c^3-2Mr_H^2=0.
\]

Introducing the dimensionless variables

\[
x=\frac{r_H}{2M},
\qquad
\beta=\frac{r_c}{2M},
\]

the horizon equation reduces to

\[
x^3-x^2+\beta^3=0.
\]

The cubic therefore follows directly from:
\begin{itemize}
    \item static spherical symmetry,
    \item Schwarzschild asymptotics,
    \item regular central structure,
    \item one intrinsic correction scale,
    \item admissible horizon interpolation.
\end{itemize}

This establishes the geometric reconstruction of the TIG horizon sector within the effective framework.

\section{Critical Branch Structure}

Define

\[
P(x,\beta)=x^3-x^2+\beta^3.
\]

The discriminant is

\[
\Delta(\beta)
=
\beta^3(4-27\beta^3).
\]

The critical point satisfies

\[
P(x_c,\beta_c)=0,
\qquad
\partial_xP(x_c,\beta_c)=0.
\]

This yields

\[
x_c=\frac{2}{3},
\qquad
\beta_c=\left(\frac{4}{27}\right)^{1/3}.
\]

The admissible branch structure therefore contains:
\begin{itemize}
    \item an outer Schwarzschild-connected branch,
    \item an inner admissible branch,
    \item a degenerate critical merger point,
    \item a horizonless supercritical sector.
\end{itemize}

The critical point corresponds to a saddle-node branch merger.

Near the critical regime, the branch separation obeys

\[
x_\pm-x_c
\sim
(\beta_c-\beta)^{1/2},
\]

demonstrating universal square-root scaling behavior.

\section{Photon Sphere Structure}

Null geodesic trajectories are governed by the effective potential

\[
V_{\mathrm{eff}}(r)
=
\frac{L^2}{r^2}F(r).
\]

Circular null geodesics satisfy

\[
rF'(r)-2F(r)=0.
\]

For Schwarzschild geometry, this reproduces

\[
r_{\mathrm{ph}}=3M.
\]

Within the TIG effective sector, the modified lapse structure induces corrections to the photon-sphere geometry near the critical regime

\[
\beta\sim\beta_c.
\]

The photon sphere therefore remains continuously connected to the admissible horizon sector.

The leading TIG correction shifts the photon sphere inward relative to the Schwarzschild geometry. In the asymptotic regime, the correction remains perturbatively small, while near criticality the deviation increases continuously.

The photon-sphere structure therefore provides one of the primary geometric observables associated with the TIG horizon sector.

\section{Curvature Invariants}

The effective geometric sector remains curvature-regular near the central region.

Near the origin,

\[
F(r)
\approx
1
-
\frac{2M}{r_c^3}r^2,
\]

which corresponds to an effective de Sitter-type structure.

The Ricci scalar therefore remains finite:

\[
R
\rightarrow
\frac{24M}{r_c^3}.
\]

The quadratic Ricci invariant remains finite:

\[
R_{\mu\nu}R^{\mu\nu}
=
\frac{144M^2}{r_c^6}.
\]

The Kretschmann scalar also remains finite:

\[
K
=
\frac{96M^2}{r_c^6}.
\]

For large radius, the geometry approaches the Schwarzschild curvature sector.

The effective geometry therefore preserves:
\begin{itemize}
    \item asymptotic Schwarzschild behavior,
    \item finite curvature invariants,
    \item regular central structure.
\end{itemize}

The present work restricts itself to the geometric consistency of this regularized sector and does not establish complete nonlinear tensor closure.
