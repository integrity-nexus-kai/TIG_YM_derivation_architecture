# Publication Strategy — TIG3 Derivation Architecture

## Purpose

This document defines the publication and positioning strategy of the TIG3 derivation architecture.

The objective is the controlled development of a mathematically governed operator-theoretic research environment while preserving:

- scientific clarity,
- admissibility-first reasoning,
- mathematical consistency,
- semantic stability,
- and publication compatibility.

---

# Strategic Principle

The repository follows the principle:

```text
stabilization before publication
```

The framework is intentionally developed through:

- controlled mathematical hardening,
- governance-oriented consolidation,
- notation stabilization,
- and claim-discipline enforcement

before public publication escalation.

---

# Current Position

The repository is currently positioned as:

- an exploratory mathematical-physics architecture,
- an operator-theoretic derivation environment,
- a spectral admissibility investigation framework,
- and a governance-controlled research layer.

The repository is NOT currently positioned as:

- a completed proof framework,
- a completed gauge-theory solution,
- a finalized unification architecture,
- or an experimentally verified physical theory.

---

# Publication Philosophy

The repository intentionally separates:

- canonical structures,
- exploratory derivations,
- internal stress-testing,
- unresolved mathematical risks,
- and publication-oriented stabilization layers.

This separation exists to preserve:

- mathematical discipline,
- semantic clarity,
- publication stability,
- and long-term governance consistency.

---

# Canonical vs Exploratory Layers

## Canonical Public Layer

The canonical public layer includes:

```text
governance/
operators/
paper/
tig3-vacuum/
```

These directories contain:

- stabilized notation,
- operator architecture,
- publication-oriented derivations,
- manuscript structures,
- and governance-controlled mathematical organization.

Canonical structures should remain:

- mathematically conservative,
- admissibility-preserving,
- semantically stable,
- and publication-compatible.

---

## Internal Exploratory Layer

The exploratory layer includes:

```text
internal/
```

This layer contains:

- unresolved operator investigations,
- compatibility stress tests,
- speculative derivation attempts,
- perturbative instability analysis,
- and exploratory mathematical structures.

Internal files are not publication-grade unless explicitly migrated into canonical public layers after governance review.
