# Purpose

This document defines the canonical interpretation boundaries of core TIG terminology.

Its purpose is to preserve terminological consistency, prevent semantic drift, and maintain rigorous structural interpretation across all TIG layers.

---

# Core Principle

Within TIG:

\[
\text{terminology must never exceed derivation}.
\]

No term may imply stronger mathematical structure than explicitly established.

---

# Canonical Interpretation Classes

TIG terminology is divided into four classes:

1. primitive structural definitions,
2. admissibility terminology,
3. effective operator-oriented terminology,
4. forbidden theorematic terminology.

---

# Class I — Primitive Structural Definitions

These terms define foundational TIG objects.

They are interpreted literally.

Includes:

- admissible state
- branch
- structural parameter
- critical transition
- discriminant geometry

These terms may appear without additional qualification.

---

# Class II — Admissibility Terminology

These terms describe effective structural restrictions.

They are interpreted structurally rather than theorematically.

Includes:

- asymptotic restriction
- finite propagation
- infrared suppression
- accessibility bounds
- structural stability
- mode separation

These terms must remain explicitly admissibility-oriented.

---

# Class III — Effective Operator Terminology

These terms are currently heuristic structural approximations.

They are NOT rigorous operator-theoretic statements.

Includes:

- compactness
- rigidity
- spectral threshold
- operator compression
- asymptotic domains
- semiflow structure

Such terminology must always remain:

- effective,
- structural,
- asymptotic,
- and non-theorematic.

---

# Class IV — Restricted Theorematic Terminology

The following terms require rigorous derivation before unrestricted usage:

- mass gap
- confinement
- spectral theorem
- compact operator
- operator algebra
- Hilbert-space closure
- quantization
- renormalization theorem

These terms may currently appear only:

- comparatively,
- negatively,
- or inside explicit limitation statements.

---

# Terminology Escalation Rule

No term may migrate upward into a stronger mathematical category without:

1. explicit derivation,
2. dependency consistency,
3. rigorous definition,
4. and canonical governance review.

---

# Semantic Drift Prohibition

The following are forbidden:

- reinterpretation of earlier definitions,
- retroactive strengthening of terminology,
- mixing heuristic and theorematic language,
- or importing external mathematical meaning without qualification.

---

# Canonical Reading Rule

All TIG terminology must currently be interpreted as:

- structurally admissibility-oriented,
- asymptotic,
- heuristic where necessary,
- and minimally committal.

Unless explicitly proven otherwise.

---

# Current Status

TIG currently defines:

- a structural admissibility architecture,
- effective asymptotic restriction principles,
- and operator-oriented structural hypotheses.

It does NOT yet define:

- rigorous operator theory,
- Yang–Mills theorems,
- spectral quantization,
- or functional-analytic closure.
