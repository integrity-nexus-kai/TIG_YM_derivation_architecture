# Purpose

This document defines the canonical structural principles governing operator-related terminology and admissibility logic within TIG.

The objective is to preserve mathematical consistency, terminological minimality, and structural coherence across the operator-theory layer.

---

# Core Principle

TIG operator terminology is interpreted structurally rather than as a completed functional-analytic framework.

Unless rigorously derived, operator-related terminology remains:

- heuristic,
- structural,
- admissibility-oriented,
- and non-theorematic.

---

# Canonical Interpretation Rule

The following terms are interpreted only in an effective structural sense unless explicitly proven otherwise:

- compactness
- rigidity
- spectral restriction
- asymptotic stability
- propagation bounds
- mode separation
- structural gaps

These terms do NOT imply formal operator-theoretic theorems by default.

---

# Admissibility Priority Principle

Within TIG:

\[
\text{admissibility precedes operator interpretation}.
\]

This means:

- admissibility constraints define the allowed structural sectors,
- operator terminology is secondary,
- and no operator structure is assumed beyond admissible effective evolution.

---

# Minimal Claim Principle

Operator terminology must never exceed the rigor of the underlying structural derivation.

In particular:

- heuristic operator language must remain explicitly marked,
- theorematic implications must not be implied prematurely,
- and speculative spectral interpretations must remain separated from proven structure.

---

# Structural Interpretation Principle

TIG currently treats operator-related terminology as:

- asymptotic structural behavior,
- admissibility-compatible evolution,
- and effective propagation organization.

It does NOT yet define:

- a complete operator algebra,
- Hilbert-space closure,
- rigorous spectral theory,
- or Yang–Mills quantization.

---

# Dependency Consistency Principle

New operator-theory documents must:

1. reference existing admissibility structure,
2. avoid introducing undefined mathematical terminology,
3. preserve dependency minimality,
4. avoid speculative theorem escalation,
5. maintain asymptotic structural interpretation.

---

# Terminology Restriction Principle

The following terms require explicit rigorous derivation before unrestricted usage:

- compact operator
- spectral theorem
- rigidity theorem
- mass gap
- confinement
- operator algebra
- quantization
- Hilbert-space completeness

Until then, such terminology may only appear:

- heuristically,
- comparatively,
- or structurally qualified.

---

# Canonical Governance Rule

TIG evolves through:

\[
\text{definition}
\rightarrow
\text{admissibility}
\rightarrow
\text{asymptotic structure}
\rightarrow
\text{operator interpretation}
\]

and NOT through speculative theorem escalation.

---

# Current Limitation

TIG currently provides:

- a structural admissibility architecture,
- asymptotic restriction principles,
- and effective operator-oriented hypotheses.

It does NOT yet provide:

- rigorous operator theory,
- Yang–Mills proofs,
- quantum gravity completion,
- or spectral theorems.

---

# Structural Priority

The primary objective of TIG is currently:

- structural consistency,
- admissibility coherence,
- asymptotic control,
- and mathematical minimality.

Not maximal theoretical scope.
