# Purpose

This document defines the canonical admissibility axioms governing the current TIG structural framework.

Its purpose is to consolidate the minimal admissibility assumptions required for consistent asymptotic structural interpretation.

---

# Core Principle

TIG currently evolves through admissibility constraints rather than unrestricted dynamical freedom.

Admissibility determines which structural sectors remain compatible with bounded asymptotic evolution.

---

# Axiom A1 — Structural Admissibility

A structural configuration is admissible only if its asymptotic evolution remains structurally bounded.

Configurations generating uncontrolled asymptotic divergence are considered non-admissible.

---

# Axiom A2 — Critical Compatibility

Admissible configurations must remain compatible with discriminant-controlled critical structure.

In particular:

\[
\Delta = \beta^3(4 - 27\beta^3)
\]

defines the canonical critical admissibility boundary currently used in TIG.

---

# Axiom A3 — Asymptotic Restriction

Admissibility may dynamically restrict asymptotic accessibility.

This restriction is currently interpreted structurally and not theorematically.

No rigorous asymptotic theorem is currently implied.

---

# Axiom A4 — Infrared Compatibility

Admissible configurations preserve bounded infrared-compatible asymptotic evolution.

Non-admissible sectors may become structurally inaccessible under critical compression.

---

# Axiom A5 — Structural Stability Priority

Within TIG:

\[
\text{stability follows admissibility}
\]

and not the reverse.

Structural admissibility precedes all operator-oriented interpretation.

---

# Axiom A6 — Minimal Structural Interpretation

Admissibility currently implies only:

- bounded asymptotic organization,
- restricted structural accessibility,
- admissibility-compatible propagation,
- and effective asymptotic stabilization.

It does NOT currently imply:

- confinement,
- mass generation,
- spectral gaps,
- or rigorous operator closure.

---

# Axiom A7 — Dependency Preservation

Admissibility assumptions may never violate lower-layer structural definitions.

All admissibility interpretation must remain dependency-compatible with:

- primitive definitions,
- derivation structure,
- and critical geometry.

---

# Axiom A8 — Non-Theorematic Operator Interpretation

All operator-oriented admissibility terminology currently remains:

- structural,
- asymptotic,
- heuristic where necessary,
- and governance-restricted.

No rigorous operator theory is currently canonical.

---

# Axiom A9 — Drift Prevention

Admissibility language must never silently escalate into:

- physical law claims,
- Yang–Mills proof claims,
- quantum gravity completion,
- or rigorous spectral interpretation.

All such extensions require explicit dependency closure.

---

# Canonical Current Status

The current TIG admissibility framework provides:

- structural admissibility organization,
- asymptotic restriction hypotheses,
- and operator-oriented extension candidates.

It does NOT yet provide:

- rigorous asymptotic theorems,
- spectral completeness,
- confinement,
- or Yang–Mills equivalence.

---

# Current Objective

The present role of admissibility within TIG is:

- structural control,
- asymptotic stabilization,
- dependency regulation,
- and theorem-oriented consistency preservation.
