# Purpose

This document defines the canonical interpretation rules governing asymptotic terminology and asymptotic structural reasoning within TIG.

Its purpose is to prevent uncontrolled extrapolation from effective asymptotic behavior toward unsupported theorematic conclusions.

---

# Core Principle

Within TIG:

\[
\text{asymptotic behavior does not automatically imply theorematic structure}
\]

All asymptotic interpretation currently remains:

- structural,
- admissibility-oriented,
- effective,
- and governance-restricted.

---

# Canonical Asymptotic Scope

TIG currently permits asymptotic interpretation only in the following sense:

- bounded structural evolution,
- admissibility-compatible restriction,
- effective infrared organization,
- asymptotic accessibility control,
- and critical structural compression.

No complete asymptotic theory is currently canonical.

---

# Canonical Infrared Interpretation

Infrared terminology currently refers only to:

- effective low-energy asymptotic organization,
- admissibility-compatible structural accessibility,
- and asymptotic restriction candidates.

It does NOT currently imply:

- confinement,
- rigorous mass generation,
- or proven spectral separation.

---

# Canonical Compression Interpretation

Critical compression currently refers only to:

- branch degeneracy behavior,
- asymptotic restriction tendencies,
- and effective accessibility reduction.

It does NOT imply physical collapse mechanisms or complete operator compression theorems.

---

# Canonical Stability Interpretation

Structural stability currently refers only to:

- admissibility-compatible bounded evolution,
- effective asymptotic regularity,
- and non-divergent structural continuation.

It does NOT imply Lyapunov completeness, rigorous stability theorems, or spectral stability proofs.

---

# Canonical Propagation Interpretation

Finite propagation terminology currently refers only to:

- effective bounded structural transmission,
- admissibility-compatible asymptotic continuation,
- and restricted structural accessibility.

It does NOT currently imply rigorous causal propagation theorems.

---

# Canonical Spectral Interpretation

Spectral terminology currently remains:

- heuristic,
- structural,
- asymptotic,
- and non-theorematic.

Terms such as:

- spectral threshold,
- infrared restriction,
- effective gap,
- or mode separation

must never be interpreted as rigorous spectral theory unless explicitly proven.

---

# Canonical Accessibility Principle

Accessibility terminology refers only to:

- admissibility-compatible structural continuation,
- effective asymptotic reachability,
- and bounded structural sectors.

It does NOT imply physical state-space completeness.

---

# Canonical Minimality Principle

Asymptotic interpretation must remain:

- minimally committal,
- dependency-compatible,
- structurally controlled,
- and mathematically conservative.

Interpretation must never exceed available derivation.

---

# Forbidden Asymptotic Escalation

The following transitions are forbidden without rigorous derivation:

- asymptotic restriction → confinement
- effective gap → mass gap
- structural compression → quantization
- infrared restriction → Yang–Mills completion
- bounded evolution → physical proof

---

# Dependency Preservation Rule

All asymptotic interpretation must remain compatible with:

- primitive definitions,
- derivation structure,
- admissibility axioms,
- and governance terminology restrictions.

No asymptotic statement may override lower-layer definitions.

---

# Current Status

The current TIG framework provides:

- asymptotic structural hypotheses,
- admissibility-oriented restriction principles,
- and effective infrared interpretation candidates.

It does NOT yet provide:

- rigorous asymptotic theorems,
- spectral completeness,
- operator closure,
- or Yang–Mills proof structure.

---

# Current Objective

The present asymptotic framework serves:

- structural stabilization,
- admissibility organization,
- dependency preservation,
- and theorem-oriented maturation.

Not unrestricted physical interpretation.
