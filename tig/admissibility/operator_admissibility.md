# Purpose

This document defines the hypothesis that admissible TIG geometries must remain compatible with structurally bounded operator behavior.

The objective is to formalize admissibility constraints for effective operator sectors.

---

# Structural Motivation

Critical geometric systems may generate uncontrolled operator amplification.

Without admissibility restrictions, this may induce:

- divergent propagation sectors,
- unstable operator behavior,
- non-compact structural evolution,
- or uncontrolled spectral growth.

TIG proposes that admissibility constrains such sectors.

---

# Operator Admissibility Hypothesis

An admissible TIG configuration must preserve structurally bounded operator compatibility.

This means:

- admissible geometric sectors remain operator-compatible,
- nonlinear branch deformation remains structurally controllable,
- and effective operator behavior avoids unrestricted divergence.

---

# Effective Interpretation

Operator admissibility is interpreted structurally.

The framework does NOT assume:

- a complete Hilbert-space formulation,
- quantum operator quantization,
- canonical commutation relations,
- or a rigorous spectral theorem.

Instead, admissibility constrains the allowed effective structural sectors.
