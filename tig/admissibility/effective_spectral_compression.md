# Purpose

This document defines the hypothesis that admissible TIG structures may induce effective spectral compression near critical geometric regimes.

The objective is to characterize how admissibility constraints may reduce effective propagation sectors.

---

# Structural Motivation

Near criticality:

\[
\Delta \rightarrow 0,
\]

branch compression increases while nonlinear coupling strengthens.

Without admissibility constraints, this may induce:

- unrestricted spectral spreading,
- unstable asymptotic propagation,
- or uncontrolled effective operator growth.

TIG proposes that admissibility restricts such sectors.

# Effective Spectral Compression Hypothesis

An admissible TIG configuration preserves structurally compressed effective spectral behavior.

This means:

- admissible propagation sectors remain bounded,
- effective structural modes remain constrained,
- and nonlinear deformation does not generate unrestricted spectral expansion.
