# Purpose

This document defines the hypothesis that admissible TIG configurations induce critical operator compression near discriminant-degenerate regimes.

The objective is to characterize dynamically restricted admissible operator sectors.

---

# Structural Motivation

Near criticality:

\[
\Delta \rightarrow 0,
\]

branch separation decreases while nonlinear interaction increases.

Without admissibility constraints, this may induce:

- unrestricted operator expansion,
- asymptotic instability,
- or uncontrolled propagation growth.

TIG proposes that admissibility dynamically compresses effective operator sectors.

---

# Critical Operator Compression Hypothesis

An admissible TIG configuration preserves structurally compressed operator evolution near criticality.

This means:

- admissible operator sectors remain bounded,
- effective structural propagation compresses dynamically,
- and nonlinear amplification remains asymptotically controllable.

---

# Effective Interpretation

Operator compression is interpreted structurally.

The framework does NOT assume:

- rigorous compact operators,
- Hilbert-space completeness,
- quantum gauge quantization,
- or full spectral closure.

Instead, admissibility constrains effective structural evolution sectors.

---

# Critical Compression Regime

Near discriminant collapse:

- admissible branch sectors compress,
- spectral freedom decreases,
- and admissible propagation becomes dynamically restricted.

Admissibility may therefore reduce effective operator dimensionality asymptotically.

---

# Structural Stability Principle

TIG assumes:

Only structurally admissible sectors preserve bounded compressed operator evolution.

Non-admissible sectors become dynamically unstable and are excluded.
