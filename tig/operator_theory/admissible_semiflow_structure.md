# Purpose

This document defines the hypothesis that admissible TIG configurations generate structurally admissible semiflow evolution near critical regimes.

The objective is to characterize bounded effective structural evolution.

---

# Structural Motivation

Admissible operator sectors must not only remain bounded statically.

They must also evolve consistently under admissible structural deformation.

Without admissibility constraints, this may induce:

- unstable propagation,
- irreversible divergence,
- asymptotic breakdown,
- or uncontrolled structural amplification.

TIG proposes that admissibility constrains effective evolution itself.

---

# Admissible Semiflow Hypothesis

An admissible TIG configuration preserves structurally admissible semiflow evolution.

This means:

- admissible evolution remains asymptotically bounded,
- effective structural propagation remains controllable,
- and nonlinear branch interaction does not generate unrestricted dynamical divergence.

---

# Effective Interpretation

Semiflow structure is interpreted structurally.

The framework does NOT assume:

- rigorous dynamical systems theory,
- complete semigroup construction,
- full operator algebra closure,
- or a quantum time-evolution operator.

Instead, admissibility constrains effective structural evolution sectors.
