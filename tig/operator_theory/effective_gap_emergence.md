# Purpose

This document defines the hypothesis that admissible TIG configurations may induce emergent effective scale separation near critical asymptotic regimes.

The objective is to characterize dynamically restricted admissible structural scales.

---

# Structural Motivation

Near criticality:

\[
\Delta \rightarrow 0,
\]

operator compression, infrared restriction, and state-sector reduction increase simultaneously.

Without admissibility constraints, this may induce:

- unrestricted asymptotic scaling,
- infinite effective propagation sectors,
- or uncontrolled infrared amplification.

TIG proposes that admissibility dynamically restricts effective structural scales.

---

# Effective Gap Emergence Hypothesis

An admissible TIG configuration may induce finite effective structural scale separation.

This means:

- admissible asymptotic sectors become dynamically compressed,
- effective propagation scales remain bounded,
- and long-range amplification remains structurally restricted.

---

Effective gap emergence is interpreted structurally.

The framework does NOT assume:

- a rigorous Yang–Mills mass gap,
- spectral quantization,
- particle mass generation,
- or a complete infrared confinement theorem.

Instead, admissibility constrains effective asymptotic scale sectors.
