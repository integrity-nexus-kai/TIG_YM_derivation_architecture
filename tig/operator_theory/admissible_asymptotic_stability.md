# Purpose

This document defines the hypothesis that admissible TIG configurations preserve stable asymptotic structural evolution near critical infrared-compatible regimes.

The objective is to characterize bounded admissible asymptotic stability behavior.

---

# Structural Motivation

Near criticality:

\[
\Delta \rightarrow 0,
\]

infrared suppression, finite propagation, and asymptotic compactness increase simultaneously.

Without admissibility constraints, this may induce:

- unstable asymptotic amplification,
- divergent long-range evolution,
- or asymptotic structural destabilization.

TIG proposes that admissibility dynamically stabilizes asymptotic structural evolution.

---

# Asymptotic Stability Hypothesis

An admissible TIG configuration preserves bounded asymptotic structural stability.

This means:

- admissible long-range evolution remains stable,
- unstable asymptotic amplification is dynamically suppressed,
- and divergent structural sectors are excluded.
