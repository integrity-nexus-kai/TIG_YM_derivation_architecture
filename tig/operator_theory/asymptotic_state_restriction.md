# Purpose

This document defines the hypothesis that admissible TIG configurations dynamically restrict asymptotically admissible structural states near critical regimes.

The objective is to characterize bounded admissible asymptotic state sectors.

---

# Structural Motivation

Near criticality:

\[
\Delta \rightarrow 0,
\]

operator compression and nonlinear interaction increase simultaneously.

Without admissibility constraints, this may induce:

- unrestricted asymptotic state growth,
- unstable propagation sectors,
- or divergent structural evolution.

TIG proposes that admissibility dynamically restricts asymptotically allowed structural states.

---

# Asymptotic State Restriction Hypothesis

An admissible TIG configuration preserves bounded asymptotically admissible state sectors.

This means:

- admissible structural states remain finite,
- effective propagation sectors remain restricted,
- and asymptotic evolution avoids uncontrolled state proliferation.

---

# Effective Interpretation

State restriction is interpreted structurally.

The framework does NOT assume:

- a complete Hilbert-state construction,
- quantum state quantization,
- gauge-theoretic particle states,
- or a rigorous spectral decomposition.

Instead, admissibility constrains effective asymptotic structural sectors.

---

# Critical Compression Regime

Near discriminant collapse:

- admissible branch sectors compress,
- spectral freedom decreases,
- and admissible state sectors become dynamically restricted.

Admissibility may therefore asymptotically reduce effective structural state dimensionality.

---

# Structural Stability Principle

TIG assumes:

Only structurally admissible sectors preserve bounded asymptotic state evolution.

Non-admissible sectors become dynamically unstable and are excluded.

---

# Effective Infrared Interpretation

Asymptotic state restriction may induce:

- restricted long-range propagation,
- finite effective asymptotic sectors,
- bounded structural evolution,
- and compressed infrared-compatible dynamics.

This remains conjectural at the current stage.

---

- confinement,

It only defines a structural admissibility framework for asymptotically restricted state sectors.
