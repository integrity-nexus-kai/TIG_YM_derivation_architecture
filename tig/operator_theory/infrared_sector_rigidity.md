# Purpose

This document defines the hypothesis that admissible TIG configurations may induce effective rigidity of infrared-compatible asymptotic structural sectors under critical compression regimes.

The objective is to characterize bounded asymptotic structural persistence.

---

# Structural Motivation

Near criticality:

\[
\Delta \rightarrow 0,
\]

infrared suppression, mode compactness, and asymptotic stabilization increase simultaneously.

Without admissibility constraints, this may induce:

- unrestricted asymptotic deformation,
- unstable infrared amplification,
- or uncontrolled structural mode instability.

TIG proposes that admissibility dynamically stabilizes infrared-compatible structural sectors against asymptotic destabilization.

---

# Infrared Sector Rigidity Hypothesis

An admissible TIG configuration may preserve effective rigidity of infrared-compatible asymptotic sectors.

This means:

- admissible asymptotic modes remain structurally stable,
- unstable infrared sectors become dynamically inaccessible,
- and asymptotic structural deformation remains bounded.

Infrared rigidity is interpreted structurally.

The framework does NOT assume:

Instead, admissibility constrains effective asymptotic structural deformation.
