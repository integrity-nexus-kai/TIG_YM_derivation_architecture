# Purpose

This document defines the hypothesis that admissible TIG configurations may induce effective structural separation between stable admissible sectors and asymptotically unstable non-admissible sectors.

The objective is to characterize bounded admissible structural evolution.

---

# Structural Motivation

Near criticality:

\[
\Delta \rightarrow 0,
\]

infrared suppression, finite propagation, and asymptotic stability increase simultaneously.

Without admissibility constraints, this may induce:

- unrestricted infrared amplification,
- asymptotic instability,
- or uncontrolled structural propagation.

TIG proposes that admissibility dynamically separates stable admissible sectors from unstable divergent sectors.

---

# Effective Structural Gap Hypothesis

An admissible TIG configuration may preserve an effective structural separation between admissible and non-admissible asymptotic sectors.

This means:

- admissible sectors remain bounded,
- unstable sectors become dynamically excluded,
- and effective asymptotic structural evolution remains finite.

---

Structural gap behavior is interpreted structurally.

The framework does NOT assume:

Instead, admissibility constrains effective asymptotic sector accessibility.
