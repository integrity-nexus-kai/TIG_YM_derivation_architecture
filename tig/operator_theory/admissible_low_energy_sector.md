# Purpose

This document defines the hypothesis that admissible TIG configurations dynamically restrict asymptotically admissible low-energy structural sectors.

The objective is to characterize bounded infrared-compatible low-energy propagation behavior.

---

# Structural Motivation

Near criticality:

\[
\Delta \rightarrow 0,
\]

infrared restriction, spectral compression, and asymptotic compactness increase simultaneously.

Without admissibility constraints, this may induce:

- unrestricted low-energy propagation,
- divergent infrared amplification,
- or uncontrolled asymptotic structural growth.

TIG proposes that admissibility dynamically restricts such low-energy sectors.

# Low-Energy Admissibility Hypothesis

An admissible TIG configuration preserves bounded infrared-compatible low-energy structural sectors.

This means:

- admissible low-energy propagation remains restricted,
- effective asymptotic amplification remains finite,
- and structurally unstable infrared sectors are excluded.

---

# Effective Interpretation

Low-energy admissibility is interpreted structurally.

The framework does NOT assume:

- particle mass generation,
- quantum gauge confinement,
- rigorous infrared decay,
- or a proven Yang–Mills gap structure.

Instead, admissibility constrains effective asymptotic low-energy sectors.
