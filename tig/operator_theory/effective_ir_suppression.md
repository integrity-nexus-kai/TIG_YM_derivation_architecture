# Purpose

This document defines the hypothesis that admissible TIG configurations dynamically suppress unstable asymptotic infrared amplification near critical structural regimes.

The objective is to characterize bounded infrared-compatible asymptotic propagation.

---

# Structural Motivation

Near criticality:

\[
\Delta \rightarrow 0,
\]

spectral compression, asymptotic compactness, and low-energy restriction increase simultaneously.

Without admissibility constraints, this may induce:

- unrestricted infrared amplification,
- divergent asymptotic propagation,
- or uncontrolled long-range structural growth.

TIG proposes that admissibility dynamically suppresses such infrared instability.

---

# Effective Infrared Suppression Hypothesis

An admissible TIG configuration preserves bounded infrared-compatible asymptotic evolution.

This means:

- unstable infrared amplification becomes dynamically restricted,
- admissible low-energy propagation remains finite,
- and long-range asymptotic divergence is structurally suppressed.
