# Purpose

This document defines the hypothesis that admissible TIG configurations may induce effective asymptotic compactness of infrared-compatible structural sectors.

The objective is to characterize bounded admissible asymptotic propagation domains.

---

# Structural Motivation

Near criticality:

\[
\Delta \rightarrow 0,
\]

spectral restriction, operator compression, and infrared admissibility increase simultaneously.

Without admissibility constraints, this may induce:

- unrestricted asymptotic propagation,
- divergent infrared sectors,
- or uncontrolled structural amplification.

TIG proposes that admissibility dynamically compresses asymptotic structural sectors into bounded effective domains.

# Asymptotic Sector Compactness Hypothesis

An admissible TIG configuration may preserve effectively compact asymptotic structural sectors.

This means:

- admissible asymptotic propagation remains bounded,
- infrared-compatible sectors remain dynamically restricted,
- and effective structural amplification remains finite.

Compactness is interpreted structurally.

The framework does NOT assume:

- rigorous functional compactness,
- compact operator spectra,
- Hilbert-space compact embeddings,
- or a proven Yang–Mills mass gap.

Instead, admissibility constrains effective asymptotic structural sectors.
