# Purpose

This document defines the hypothesis that admissible TIG structures induce asymptotically bounded effective operator behavior.

The objective is to characterize structurally admissible operator evolution near critical regimes.

---

# Structural Motivation

Near criticality:

\[
\Delta \rightarrow 0,
\]

nonlinear branch interaction and spectral compression increase simultaneously.

Without admissibility constraints, this may induce:

- unrestricted operator amplification,
- asymptotic instability,
- or divergent structural propagation.

TIG proposes that admissibility constrains such behavior.

---

# Asymptotic Operator Bound Hypothesis

An admissible TIG configuration preserves asymptotically bounded effective operator behavior.

This means:

- admissible operator evolution remains structurally finite,
- nonlinear structural amplification remains bounded,
- and asymptotic propagation does not diverge uncontrollably.

---

# Effective Interpretation

Operator bounds are interpreted structurally.

The framework does NOT assume:

- a complete Hilbert-space formulation,
- rigorous self-adjoint operators,
- quantum operator quantization,
- or a complete spectral theorem.

Instead, admissibility constrains effective structural evolution sectors.

---

# Critical Compression Regime

Near discriminant collapse:

- branch compression increases,
- admissible spectral sectors shrink,
- and effective propagation becomes restricted.

Admissibility may therefore dynamically bound asymptotic operator evolution.

---

# Structural Stability Principle

TIG assumes:

Only structurally admissible sectors preserve bounded asymptotic operator behavior.

Non-admissible sectors become asymptotically unstable and are excluded.

---

# Effective Infrared Interpretation

Asymptotic operator bounds may induce:

- restricted infrared propagation,
- finite effective structural sectors,
- bounded asymptotic evolution,
- and compressed admissible dynamics.

This remains conjectural at the current stage.

---

# Current Limitation

It only defines a structural admissibility framework for asymptotically bounded operator behavior.
