# Purpose

This document defines the hypothesis that admissible TIG configurations may induce effective separation between stable infrared-compatible modes and unstable divergent asymptotic modes.

---

# Structural Motivation

Near criticality:

\[
\Delta \rightarrow 0,
\]

admissible sectors compress while unstable long-range propagation becomes increasingly restricted.

# Infrared Mode Separation Hypothesis

An admissible TIG configuration may preserve an effective separation between:

- admissible nondivergent infrared modes,
- and non-admissible divergent asymptotic modes.

This means that stable long-range behavior remains structurally restricted while divergent propagation sectors are excluded.

---

# Effective Interpretation

Infrared mode separation is interpreted structurally.

The framework does NOT assume:

- rigorous spectral decomposition,
- particle mass generation,
- Yang–Mills confinement,
- or a proven mass gap.

Instead, admissibility constrains which asymptotic modes remain structurally accessible.

# Critical Compression Regime

Near discriminant collapse:

- admissible propagation sectors compress,
- structural instability of divergent modes increases,
- and infrared-compatible modes remain bounded.

Admissibility may therefore separate stable effective modes from unstable asymptotic sectors.

---

# Structural Stability Principle

TIG assumes:

Only structurally admissible infrared modes preserve bounded asymptotic evolution.

Non-admissible divergent modes become asymptotically unstable and are excluded.
