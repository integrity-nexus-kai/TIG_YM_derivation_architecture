# Purpose

This document defines the hypothesis that admissible TIG configurations may induce bounded asymptotic accessibility between structurally stable infrared-compatible sectors and asymptotically unstable non-admissible sectors.

The objective is to characterize admissible asymptotic structural reachability.

---

# Structural Motivation

Near criticality:

\[
\Delta \rightarrow 0,
\]

infrared suppression, spectral restriction, and asymptotic stabilization increase simultaneously.

Without admissibility constraints, this may induce:

- unrestricted asymptotic accessibility,
- unstable infrared amplification,
- or uncontrolled structural mode proliferation.

TIG proposes that admissibility dynamically restricts asymptotic structural accessibility.

---

# Asymptotic Accessibility Hypothesis

An admissible TIG configuration may preserve bounded asymptotic accessibility between structurally admissible sectors.

This means:

- admissible infrared-compatible sectors remain structurally reachable,
- unstable asymptotic sectors become dynamically inaccessible,
- and asymptotic structural evolution remains bounded.

---

# Effective Interpretation

Accessibility bounds are interpreted structurally.

The framework does NOT assume:

- rigorous spectral quantization,
- Yang–Mills confinement,
- particle mass generation,
- or a proven mass gap.

Instead, admissibility constrains effective asymptotic structural reachability.
