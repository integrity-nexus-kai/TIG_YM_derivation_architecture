# Purpose

This document defines the hypothesis that admissible TIG configurations may induce effective structural spectral thresholds separating bounded admissible asymptotic sectors from structurally unstable sectors.

The objective is to characterize admissible asymptotic spectral accessibility.

---

# Structural Motivation

Near criticality:

\[
\Delta \rightarrow 0,
\]

infrared suppression, asymptotic stabilization, and structural hierarchy formation increase simultaneously.

Without admissibility constraints, this may induce:

- unrestricted asymptotic spectral accessibility,
- unstable infrared amplification,
- or uncontrolled structural mode proliferation.

TIG proposes that admissibility dynamically restricts asymptotic spectral accessibility through structural threshold formation.

# Structural Spectral Threshold Hypothesis

An admissible TIG configuration may preserve effective asymptotic spectral thresholds separating:

- admissible bounded structural modes,
- and asymptotically unstable non-admissible sectors.

This means:

- structurally unstable asymptotic modes become dynamically inaccessible,
- admissible infrared-compatible propagation remains bounded,
- and asymptotic spectral accessibility becomes restricted.
