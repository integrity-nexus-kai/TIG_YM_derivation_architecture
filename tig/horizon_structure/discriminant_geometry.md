# Purpose

This document formalizes the geometric interpretation of the discriminant structure within TIG.

The purpose is to characterize how branch separation changes as the discriminant approaches zero.

---

# Structural Equation

The TIG cubic structure is

\[
x^3 - x^2 + \beta^3 = 0.
\]

Its discriminant is

\[
\Delta = \beta^3(4-27\beta^3).
\]

---

# Critical Geometry

The critical regime occurs when

\[
\Delta \rightarrow 0.
\]

At exact criticality:

\[
\Delta = 0.
\]

This defines the structural branch-degeneracy boundary.

---

# Geometric Interpretation

The discriminant is interpreted geometrically as a branch-separation measure.

Large positive discriminant:
- structurally separated branches.

Vanishing discriminant:
- branch coalescence,
- reduced structural distinguishability,
- enhanced nonlinear sensitivity.

---

# Structural Compression

Near criticality the admissible horizon structure becomes compressed in configuration space.

Small parameter variations may induce large structural branch responses.

# Degenerate Geometry

When

\[

This regime is interpreted as a degenerate geometric sector.
