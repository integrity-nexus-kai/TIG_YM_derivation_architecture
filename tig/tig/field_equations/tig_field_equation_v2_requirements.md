# Structural Requirements

A valid TIG v2 field equation must satisfy the following conditions.

---

## R1 — Natural Cubic Generation

The equation must naturally generate:

\[
x^3-x^2+\beta^3=0
\]

as an emergent horizon condition.

The cubic must not be artificially inserted afterward.

---

## R2 — Schwarzschild Compatibility

The equation must satisfy:

\[
\beta\rightarrow0
\Rightarrow
x\rightarrow1.
\]

The asymptotic Schwarzschild sector must remain intact.

---

## R3 — Critical Structure Preservation

The equation must preserve the critical TIG branch point:

\[
x_c=\frac23,
\qquad
\beta_c^3=\frac4{27}.
\]

---

## R4 — Generalized Metric Compatibility

The equation must allow generalized static spherical metrics of the form:

\[
ds^2
=
-A(r)\,dt^2
+
\frac{dr^2}{B(r)}
+
r^2d\Omega^2.
\]

The overly restrictive condition:

\[
A(r)=B(r)
\]

must not be imposed a priori.

---

## R5 — Exact Tensor Closure

The reconstructed equation must allow exact componentwise tensor closure:

\[
E_{\mu\nu}=0
\]

without residual fitting or post-defined effective cancellation sectors.

---

## R6 — Curvature Regularity

The equation must preserve:

- bounded curvature invariants,
- admissible horizon structure,
- and asymptotic decay behavior.

---

## R7 — Structural Integrity Preservation

The reconstruction program must preserve the central TIG principle:

\[

\text{Integrity is structural,
not phenomenological fitting.}
}
\]

The field equation must therefore emerge from admissibility structure rather than parameter flexibility.
