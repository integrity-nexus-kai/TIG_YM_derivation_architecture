# Core Principle

Within TIG:

\[
\text{variation must preserve admissibility}
\]

A variational structure is admissible only if its induced dynamics preserve the canonical admissibility constraints of the framework.

---

# Minimal Consistency Requirements

An admissible variational structure must preserve:

- asymptotic boundedness,
- regularity compatibility,
- branch continuity,
- discriminant compatibility,
- and admissible horizon structure.

---

# Asymptotic Consistency

The induced geometry must asymptotically approach Schwarzschild-compatible behavior for:

\[
r \gg r_c.
\]

Unbounded asymptotic deformation is non-admissible.

---

# Regularity Consistency

The induced geometry must avoid uncontrolled singular divergence within the admissible regime.

Regularization structure must remain bounded and dependency-compatible.

---

# Branch Consistency

The induced dynamics must preserve compatibility with the canonical horizon equation:

\[
x^3-x^2+\beta^3=0.
\]

The variational structure must not destroy admissible branch continuity.

---

# Critical Consistency

Near:

\[
\beta \rightarrow \beta_c,
\]

the induced structure must remain discriminant-compatible.

Admissible critical compression must remain structurally preserved.

---

# Minimal Dynamical Compatibility

The induced dynamics must remain compatible with the effective candidate structure:

\[
G_{\mu\nu}
+
\Lambda_{\mathrm{eff}}\,g_{\mu\nu}
+
\alpha\,\mathcal{R}_{\mu\nu}^{(\mathrm{corr})}
=
8\pi T_{\mu\nu}^{(\mathrm{eff})}.
\]

This compatibility currently remains structural rather than rigorously derived.

---

# Structural Interpretation

The consistency conditions currently serve only as:

- admissibility constraints,
- variational stability restrictions,
- and structural compatibility requirements.

They do NOT currently imply:

- rigorous uniqueness,
- quantum consistency,
- renormalizability,
- or Yang–Mills equivalence.

---

# Minimality Constraint

The variational consistency structure must remain:

- geometrically minimal,
- asymptotically controlled,
- structurally conservative,
- and dependency-compatible.

Unnecessary dynamical escalation is prohibited.

---

# Forbidden Escalation

The following are currently forbidden:

- presenting consistency conditions as complete proofs,
- claiming rigorous variational closure,
- claiming operator completeness,
- or claiming full gravitational completion.

---

# Weakest Step

The weakest current step is:

- the absence of rigorous action derivation,
- the absence of asymptotic completeness proofs,
- and the absence of operator closure.

---

The current TIG framework provides:

- admissibility-compatible geometric structure,

- and variational admissibility constraints.

It does NOT yet provide:

- rigorous field-equation derivation,
