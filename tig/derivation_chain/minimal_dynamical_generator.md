# Purpose

This document defines the minimal structural role of a future TIG dynamical generator.

Its purpose is not to present a completed field equation, but to specify the smallest admissible dynamical object capable of generating TIG-compatible structural behavior.

# Core Principle

Within TIG:

\[
\text{dynamics must be generated minimally from admissibility}
\]

A dynamical generator is admissible only if it preserves the canonical primitive structure and does not introduce unsupported new primitives.

---

# Minimal Generator Requirement

A minimal TIG dynamical generator must act on admissible structural configurations without violating:

- admissible horizon states,
- structural horizon branches,
- structural parameter beta,
- discriminant-controlled criticality,
- and asymptotic admissibility restriction.

---

# Canonical Generator Role

The generator must explain how admissible structural states may evolve while preserving compatibility with the canonical structural normal form:

\[
x^3 - x^2 + \beta^3 = 0
\]

It must not replace this normal form without derivation.

---

# Admissibility Preservation

A valid generator must preserve:

- bounded structural continuation,
- branch-compatible evolution,
- non-divergent asymptotic behavior,
- and critical-sector consistency.

Any generator producing uncontrolled divergence is non-admissible.

---

# Critical Compatibility

The generator must remain compatible with:

\[
\Delta = \beta^3(4 - 27\beta^3)
\]

and must not erase or bypass discriminant-controlled critical behavior.

---

# Minimality Constraint

The generator must not introduce:

- hidden dimensions,
- arbitrary auxiliary fields,
- unrestricted quantization,
- spectral assumptions,
- or Yang–Mills-specific structure.

Any additional structure requires independent derivation and governance approval.

---

# Effective Interpretation

At the current stage, the minimal dynamical generator is a structural placeholder.

It identifies the required role of dynamics but does not yet define a complete equation of motion.

---

# Weakest Step

The weakest current step is the absence of an explicit admissibility-preserving generator.

This document only defines the requirements such a generator must satisfy.
