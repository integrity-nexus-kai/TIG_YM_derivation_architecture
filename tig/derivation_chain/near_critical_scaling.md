# Canonical Horizon Equation

The canonical TIG horizon equation is:

\[
P(x,\beta)=x^3-x^2+\beta^3=0.
\]

The critical point satisfies:

\[
x_c=\frac{2}{3}
\]

and:

\[
\beta_c^3=\frac{4}{27}.
\]

---

# Near-Critical Expansion

Define small deviations:

\[
x=x_c+\varepsilon
\]

and:

\[
\beta=\beta_c-\delta.
\]

Near the critical point, the linear term in the branch expansion becomes suppressed because:

\[
\frac{\partial P}{\partial x}=0
\]

at:

\[
(x_c,\beta_c).
\]

Thus the dominant branch deformation becomes effectively quadratic in the local expansion structure.
