# Purpose

This document defines the canonical structural requirements for any future TIG field-equation candidate.

Its purpose is not to introduce a completed field equation, but to rigorously constrain which types of dynamical structures may become admissible within the TIG framework.

Within TIG:

\[

Any admissible field-equation candidate must reproduce the canonical structural constraints already established within the primitive and admissibility layers.

# Admissibility Preservation Requirement

A valid TIG dynamical structure must preserve:

- admissible horizon states,
- bounded asymptotic continuation,
- branch-compatible evolution,
- and discriminant-controlled critical structure.

Dynamics generating uncontrolled divergence are considered non-admissible.

# Minimality Requirement

A TIG field-equation candidate must remain:

- structurally minimal,
- dependency-compatible,
- asymptotically controlled,
- and mathematically conservative.

Unnecessary dynamical complexity is prohibited.

---

# Asymptotic Compatibility Requirement

Any admissible field equation must preserve:

- asymptotic boundedness,
- admissibility-compatible accessibility,
- and non-divergent structural continuation.

No rigorous asymptotic theorem is currently implied.

---

# Operator Independence Requirement

The primitive field-equation layer must remain independent of:

- spectral assumptions,
- operator closure,
- Hilbert-space structure,
- or Yang–Mills interpretation.

Operator-oriented terminology may not define primitive dynamics.

---

# Structural Reduction Requirement

Any admissible dynamical equation must reduce consistently to:

1. primitive admissibility objects,
2. branch structure,
3. critical discriminant behavior,
4. asymptotic admissibility restriction.

No hidden primitives may appear dynamically.

---

# Forbidden Dynamical Escalation

The following are currently forbidden:

- introducing arbitrary higher-dimensional structure,
- introducing unrestricted quantization,
- interpreting admissibility as complete physical law,
- or presenting heuristic dynamics as rigorous completion.

---

# Weakest Step

The weakest current step is:

- the absence of a rigorously derived dynamical generator,
- and the absence of admissibility-complete evolution equations.

This limitation remains explicit.

- a complete field equation,
- rigorous dynamical closure,
- operator completeness,
- or Yang–Mills equivalence.
