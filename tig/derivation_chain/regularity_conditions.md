# Core Principle

Within TIG:

\[
\text{admissibility requires bounded structural behavior}
\]

A geometry is admissible only if structurally relevant quantities remain sufficiently controlled within the effective regime.

---

# Effective Metric Ansatz

The current effective metric function is:

\[
F(r)=1-\frac{2Mr^2}{r^3+r_c^3}.
\]

This ansatz is interpreted structurally and not as a completed field-equation solution.

---

# Regularity Requirement

An admissible effective geometry must avoid uncontrolled divergence of structurally relevant quantities within the admissible domain.

In particular:

- branch structure must remain well-defined,
- admissible continuation must remain bounded,
- and asymptotic compatibility must remain preserved.

---

# Small-Radius Structural Behavior

For:

\[
r \ll r_c
\]

the effective metric behaves approximately as:

\[
F(r)\sim 1-\frac{2M}{r_c^3}r^2.
\]

This behavior is structurally bounded at the level of the effective metric function.

---

# Structural Interpretation

The bounded small-radius behavior currently suggests:

- admissibility-compatible compression,
- effective structural regularization,
- and restricted divergence tendency.

It does NOT currently imply:

- rigorous singularity removal,
- geodesic completeness,
- or full curvature regularity.

---

# Admissibility Compatibility

A structurally admissible geometry must preserve:

- bounded branch accessibility,
- critical discriminant compatibility,
- asymptotic consistency,
- and non-divergent effective continuation.

Configurations violating these conditions are non-admissible.

---

# Critical Compatibility

Regularity conditions must remain compatible with:

\[
\Delta=\beta^3(4-27\beta^3)

and must not invalidate the canonical critical branch structure.
