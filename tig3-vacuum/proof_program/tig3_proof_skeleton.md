# TIG3 Proof Skeleton

## Purpose

This document defines the preliminary proof architecture of the TIG3 admissibility program.

The objective of TIG3 is not to assert a completed gravitational theory, but to investigate whether the TIG2 branch structure admits mathematically consistent vacuum continuation satisfying:

- admissibility,
- bounded geometric behavior,
- asymptotic GR consistency,
- and structurally stable near-critical dynamics.

The current proof skeleton is organizational and incomplete.

---

# Foundational Starting Point

TIG3 begins from the exact TIG2 branch equation

x^3 - x^2 + beta^3 = 0

together with:

- discriminant structure,
- saddle-node criticality,
- admissible branch sectors,
- and near-critical scaling behavior.

The central mathematical question is:

Can admissible vacuum geometry consistently support the TIG2 branch structure without dynamically requiring divergent geometric continuation?

---

# Global Proof Strategy

The TIG3 proof program proceeds through the following logical sequence:

1. define admissible vacuum geometry,
2. establish admissibility constraints,
3. construct bounded-curvature sectors,
4. preserve asymptotic GR consistency,
5. analyze near-critical branch dynamics,
6. investigate regulated continuation.

The structure therefore proceeds from:

- branch geometry,
- to admissibility,
- to stability,
- to bounded continuation.

---

# Step 1 — Admissible Vacuum Geometry

## Objective

Define the admissible geometric class.

## Required Ingredients

- Lorentzian metric structure,
- admissible continuation,
- structural branch compatibility,
- and asymptotic GR recovery.

## Current Status

Partially established through TIG3 definitions.

---

# Step 2 — Minimal Metric Sector

## Objective

Construct a representative admissible metric sector suitable for structural analysis.

## Representative Geometry

The current TIG3 representative sector is given by

ds^2
=
-F(r)dt^2
+
dr^2 / F(r)
+
r^2 dOmega^2

with representative lapse structure

F(r)
=
1 - (2Mr^2)/(r^3 + r_c^3)

This geometry is representative and not claimed to be unique.

## Current Status

Working admissible metric sector established.

---

# Step 3 — Structural Control Parameter

## Objective

Interpret the parameter

beta = r_c / (2M)

as a structural deformation parameter governing admissible branch geometry.

## Required Properties

- continuity outside the critical regime,
- admissible branch deformation,
- controlled near-critical behavior,
- and asymptotic consistency.

## Current Status

Structurally defined but not dynamically derived.

---

# Step 4 — Bounded Curvature Sector

## Objective

Investigate whether admissible branch continuation may admit bounded high-curvature sectors.

## Candidate Targets

Potentially bounded quantities include:

- Kretschmann scalar,
- Ricci invariants,
- and admissible higher-order curvature structures.

## Core Difficulty

No rigorous curvature estimates currently exist.

This is a major open problem.

---

# Step 5 — GR-Compatible Continuation

## Objective

Establish continuous reduction to asymptotic GR behavior.

## Required Conditions

- Schwarzschild recovery,
- asymptotic flatness,
- weak-field consistency,
- and controlled branch deformation.

## Central Requirement

The limit

beta -> 0

must recover Schwarzschild-compatible behavior outside the regulated branch regime.

---

# Step 6 — Near-Critical Dynamics

## Objective

Analyze the regime

beta ≈ beta_c

where the branch structure becomes maximally sensitive.

## Candidate Phenomena

Possible structural effects include:

- metastable branch sectors,
- amplified relaxation dynamics,
- bounded curvature amplification,
- and near-critical stabilization.

## Current Status

Only heuristic and simulation-supported.

No rigorous dynamical theorem exists.

---

# Step 7 — Regulated Continuation

## Objective

Investigate whether admissible branch geometry dynamically favors bounded continuation over unrestricted divergent collapse.

## Important Restriction

TIG3 does not currently claim a proof of singularity exclusion.

The framework instead studies whether:

- admissibility,
- bounded curvature,
- and branch stability

may naturally constrain the collapse sector.

This distinction is essential.

The current proof architecture follows:

TIG2 branch structure

The ordering is mandatory.

# Mathematical Obstacles

The current TIG3 framework still lacks:

- a covariant field equation,
- PDE control,
- nonlinear stability analysis,
- operator-theoretic structure,
- and rigorous curvature estimates.

These remain major open mathematical problems.

---

# Simulation Role

The TIG3 simulations currently serve only heuristic purposes.

Their role is:

- visualization,
- structural exploration,
- and identification of candidate dynamical regimes.

They do not constitute proof.

---

# Excluded Claims

The TIG3 proof program does not currently claim:

- complete singularity elimination,
- quantum-gravity completion,
- observational confirmation,
- or replacement of general relativity.

The framework remains conservative and geometric.

The framework therefore studies:

- admissibility,

within a structural-consistency framework.
