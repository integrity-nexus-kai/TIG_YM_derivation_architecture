# TIG3 Theorem Candidates

## Purpose

This document classifies the current mathematical statements of the TIG3 admissibility program into:

- established calculations,
- structural propositions,
- conjectures,
- heuristic interpretations,
- and future theorem targets.

The objective is to maintain strict separation between:

```text
proven
vs
calculated
vs
hypothesized
vs
interpretive
```

statements.

This separation is essential for mathematical consistency and future publication stability.

---

# Classification Convention

The TIG3 program currently uses the following classification hierarchy:

## Level A — Explicitly Calculated

Statements derived directly from explicit calculation within the representative metric sector.

## Level B — Structural Proposition

Statements logically supported by the current framework but not yet elevated to rigorous theorem status.

## Level C — Theorem Candidate

Statements believed to be mathematically provable once additional operator/PDE structure is established.

## Level D — Structural Conjecture

Statements motivated by the framework but currently lacking rigorous derivation.

## Level E — Heuristic Interpretation

Physical or geometric interpretation not required for mathematical consistency.
