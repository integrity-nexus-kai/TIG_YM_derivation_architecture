# TIG3 Lemma Chain

## Purpose

This document defines the preliminary logical dependency structure of the TIG3 admissibility proof program.

The objective is not yet to provide a complete proof, but to organize the minimal sequence of structural statements required for a mathematically coherent TIG3 vacuum framework.

The current lemma chain is organizational and remains incomplete.

---

# Foundational Strategy

The TIG3 program proceeds in the following order:

1. establish the TIG2 branch structure,
2. define admissible vacuum geometry,
3. impose bounded-curvature conditions,
4. establish asymptotic GR consistency,
5. analyze near-critical branch dynamics,
6. investigate dynamically regulated continuation.

The proof architecture therefore moves from:

- exact branch structure,
- to admissibility,
- to stability,
- to bounded continuation.

# Preliminary Lemma Structure

## Lemma 1 — TIG2 Branch Existence

The cubic horizon equation

x^3 - x^2 + beta^3 = 0

admits a structurally well-defined branch sector characterized by:

- admissible real branches,
- discriminant structure,
- and critical saddle-node behavior.

This result is inherited from TIG2.

---

## Lemma 2 — Structural Control Parameter

The parameter

beta = r_c / (2M)

acts as a continuous structural deformation parameter governing the admissible branch sector.

The branch geometry varies continuously with beta outside the critical degeneracy point.

---

## Lemma 3 — Admissible Vacuum Continuation

There exist Lorentzian vacuum geometries compatible with the TIG2 branch structure satisfying:

- metric regularity,
- admissible continuation,
- and asymptotic GR consistency.

This statement currently remains partially conjectural.

---

## Lemma 4 — Bounded Curvature Sector

Under suitable admissibility conditions, candidate regulated core sectors may exist in which relevant geometric invariants remain finite.

Potentially bounded quantities include:

- Kretschmann scalar,
- Ricci invariants,
- and admissible higher-order curvature structures.

This lemma is currently heuristic.

---

## Lemma 5 — Near-Critical Structural Stability

Near the critical branch regime

beta ≈ beta_c

admissible branch geometries may exhibit dynamically bounded relaxation behavior instead of unrestricted divergent continuation.

Possible structural phenomena include:

- metastable branch sectors,
- bounded relaxation modes,
- and near-critical geometric stabilization.

This statement remains conjectural.

---

## Lemma 6 — GR-Compatible Regulated Continuation

Any admissible TIG3 regulated sector must continuously recover Schwarzschild-compatible asymptotic behavior away from the regulated branch region.

This condition constrains the admissible TIG class.

# Dependency Structure

The current logical dependency chain is:

TIG2 branch structure
→ admissible vacuum geometry
→ bounded curvature conditions
→ near-critical dynamics
→ regulated continuation
→ asymptotic GR consistency

The order is essential.

Near-critical stabilization cannot be studied before admissibility and boundedness are defined.

---

# Current Limitations

The current lemma chain does not yet provide:

- a covariant field equation,
- a complete dynamical proof,
- rigorous PDE control,
- nonlinear stability analysis,
- or observational validation.

The present framework remains structural and programmatic.

- admissibility,
