# Admissible TIG Vacuum Geometry

## Purpose

This document defines the foundational notion of an admissible vacuum geometry within the TIG3 structural-consistency framework.

The purpose of TIG3 is not to replace general relativity, but to investigate what geometric conditions are required in order for the TIG2 horizon branch structure to remain mathematically admissible, dynamically regular, and asymptotically compatible with the GR limit.

---

# Foundational Principle

TIG3 begins from the assumption that the TIG2 branch structure

x^3 - x^2 + beta^3 = 0

is not merely an algebraic artifact, but represents a structurally admissible horizon-sector geometry whose continuation imposes nontrivial constraints on the vacuum sector itself.

The central TIG3 question is therefore:

What class of vacuum geometries can consistently support the TIG2 branch sector without producing non-admissible geometric continuation?

---

# Preliminary Definition

An admissible TIG vacuum geometry is a Lorentzian spacetime

(M, g)

satisfying the following conditions.

---

## 1. Regular Metric Structure

The metric tensor g must remain sufficiently regular throughout the admissible sector.

At minimum:

- g must remain nondegenerate,
- the spacetime continuation must remain geometrically well-defined,
- no dynamically preferred divergent continuation may occur within the admissible branch sector.

---

## 2. TIG2 Branch Compatibility

The vacuum geometry must admit the TIG2 structural horizon branch characterized by

x^3 - x^2 + beta^3 = 0

together with:

- its discriminant structure,
- critical branch transition,
- saddle-node scaling behavior,
- and admissible near-critical continuation.

---

## 3. Structural Control Parameter

The parameter

beta = r_c / (2M)

is interpreted as a dimensionless structural control parameter governing the admissible branch sector.

Within TIG3:

- beta is not an additional spatial dimension,
- not a hidden coordinate,
- and not a free metaphysical parameter.

Instead, beta controls the structural deformation of the horizon branch geometry.

---

## 4. Bounded Curvature Sector

An admissible TIG vacuum geometry should satisfy boundedness conditions for relevant geometric invariants within the regulated sector.

Candidate conditions include bounded:

- Kretschmann scalar,
- Ricci invariants,
- and admissible metric continuation.

The precise admissibility class remains part of the TIG3 proof program.

---

## 5. GR Limit Consistency

The admissible TIG sector must continuously recover standard GR behavior in the appropriate asymptotic regime.

In particular:

beta -> 0

must recover Schwarzschild-compatible behavior away from the regulated core sector.

The GR limit is treated as a non-negotiable consistency condition.

---

# Structural Interpretation

TIG3 interprets vacuum geometry as a constrained admissibility problem rather than an unrestricted singular continuation problem.

The framework therefore investigates whether structurally admissible branch sectors dynamically favor:

- regulated high-curvature regions,
- bounded geometric continuation,
- and stable near-critical configurations,

instead of divergent singular collapse states.
