# Regularized Core Region

## Purpose

This document defines the concept of a dynamically regulated high-curvature core region within the TIG3 admissibility framework.

The objective is not to assume the elimination of singular behavior a priori, but to investigate whether admissible TIG branch geometries may dynamically favor bounded geometric continuation instead of divergent collapse states.

---

# Conceptual Motivation

Within classical GR, unrestricted collapse may produce singular geometric configurations characterized by divergent curvature invariants.

TIG3 instead investigates whether the admissible TIG2 branch structure imposes structural conditions capable of dynamically regulating the high-curvature sector.

The central question is therefore:

Can admissible branch geometries support dynamically bounded core regions without destroying asymptotic GR consistency?

---

# Preliminary Definition

A regularized core region is a connected spacetime subset

R_core ⊂ M

satisfying the following preliminary conditions.

---

## 1. Bounded Geometric Invariants

Relevant curvature invariants remain finite throughout the regulated sector.

Candidate quantities include:

- Kretschmann scalar,
- Ricci scalar,
- Ricci contraction,
- and admissible higher-order invariants.

Symbolically:

sup |I(p)| < infinity

for admissible invariant classes I.

---

## 2. Admissible Metric Continuation

The spacetime geometry must remain continuously extendable through the regulated sector.

In particular:

- no metric degeneration is dynamically preferred,
- no divergent branch continuation is structurally required,
- and the admissible branch structure remains well-defined.

---

## 3. TIG2 Branch Compatibility

The regulated core sector must remain compatible with the TIG2 branch geometry

x^3 - x^2 + beta^3 = 0

including:

- admissible branch continuation,
- discriminant structure,
- and near-critical scaling behavior.

---

## 4. Structural Stabilization

The regulated sector may admit dynamically stabilized configurations characterized by:

- bounded relaxation behavior,
- near-critical stabilization,
- or metastable geometric modes.

This does not yet constitute a dynamical theorem.

It is currently a structural admissibility hypothesis.

---

# Near-Critical Dynamics

The regime

beta ≈ beta_c

is expected to exhibit enhanced structural sensitivity.

Possible behaviors include:

- amplified relaxation times,
- quasi-periodic geometric response,
- increased branch sensitivity,
- and dynamically regulated high-curvature sectors.

The current TIG3 simulations heuristically explore this regime but do not yet provide rigorous numerical proof.

---

# Relation to Singular Collapse

TIG3 does not currently claim a proof of singularity exclusion.

Instead, the framework investigates whether admissible branch geometries dynamically favor regulated continuation over divergent collapse states.

The distinction is essential.

TIG3 therefore studies:

- admissibility,
- bounded continuation,
- and structural regularization,

rather than asserting a complete replacement of GR singularity theory.

---

# GR Limit Consistency

Any admissible regulated core sector must remain asymptotically compatible with the GR limit.

In particular:

beta -> 0

must recover Schwarzschild-compatible behavior away from the regulated sector.

---

# Status

This document defines the current working notion of a regularized TIG3 core region.

The framework remains preliminary and does not yet provide a complete covariant dynamical proof.
