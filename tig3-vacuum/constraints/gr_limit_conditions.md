# GR Limit Conditions

## Purpose

This document defines the general-relativistic consistency requirements imposed on the TIG3 vacuum framework.

The purpose of TIG3 is not to discard general relativity, but to investigate whether additional admissible branch structure may emerge in the high-curvature regime while continuously preserving the classical GR limit in the appropriate asymptotic sector.

The GR limit is treated as a mandatory structural consistency condition.

---

# Foundational Principle

Any admissible TIG3 vacuum geometry must reduce continuously to standard GR behavior outside the regulated branch sector.

The TIG framework therefore treats:

- asymptotic GR recovery,
- Schwarzschild compatibility,
- and weak-structure consistency

as non-negotiable admissibility requirements.

---

# Primary GR Consistency Conditions

## 1. Schwarzschild Recovery

The limit

beta -> 0

must continuously recover Schwarzschild-compatible behavior outside the regulated core region.

In particular, the metric sector should reduce asymptotically to:

ds^2 = -(1 - 2M/r)dt^2 + dr^2/(1 - 2M/r) + r^2 dOmega^2

away from the regulated branch regime.

---

## 2. Asymptotic Flatness

Admissible TIG3 vacuum sectors should remain asymptotically compatible with flat spacetime behavior at sufficiently large radial distance.

Candidate asymptotic conditions include:

- vanishing curvature invariants,
- asymptotically Schwarzschild geometry,
- and controlled branch deformation.

---

## 3. Weak-Structure Consistency

Far from the regulated branch sector:

- standard weak-field behavior should be recovered,
- no spurious long-range branch instabilities should appear,
- and no uncontrolled asymptotic deviations should emerge.

This condition is essential for physical admissibility.

---

## 4. Controlled Branch Deformation

The TIG structural parameter

beta = r_c / (2M)

must deform the branch geometry continuously.

Admissible TIG sectors therefore require:

- smooth asymptotic continuation,
- absence of uncontrolled branch bifurcation outside the critical regime,
- and preservation of large-scale GR consistency.

---

## 5. Near-Critical Localization

Potential TIG3 deviations from standard GR are expected to remain localized near the regulated branch sector.

In particular, the regime

beta ≈ beta_c

may produce:

- enhanced branch sensitivity,
- near-critical relaxation behavior,
- and bounded high-curvature dynamics,

without destroying asymptotic GR recovery.

---

# Excluded Interpretations

The TIG3 framework does not currently claim:

- replacement of GR,
- observational contradiction of GR,
- or elimination of classical GR validity.

Instead, TIG3 investigates whether admissible branch continuation may extend the high-curvature sector while preserving the classical asymptotic regime.

---

# Structural Interpretation

Within TIG3, the GR limit acts as a structural anchor condition.

Any admissible vacuum geometry failing to recover asymptotic GR consistency is excluded from the admissible TIG class.

The admissibility problem is therefore constrained simultaneously by:

- branch regularity,
- bounded curvature behavior,
- and asymptotic GR recovery.
