# Vacuum Admissibility Conditions

## Purpose

This document defines the preliminary admissibility conditions imposed on the TIG3 vacuum sector.

The objective is to identify structural conditions under which the TIG2 branch geometry may admit dynamically regular and asymptotically consistent vacuum continuation.

The conditions collected here are not yet formal theorems.

They define the current admissibility framework of TIG3.

---

# Foundational Principle

TIG3 treats the vacuum sector as a constrained geometric admissibility problem.

Not every mathematically possible continuation is considered physically admissible.

Instead, admissibility is restricted by:

- branch consistency,
- bounded geometric behavior,
- asymptotic compatibility,
- and structural stability.

---

# Preliminary Admissibility Conditions

## 1. Lorentzian Metric Admissibility

The spacetime geometry must remain Lorentzian throughout the admissible sector.

The metric tensor g must remain:

- nondegenerate,
- continuously extendable,
- and geometrically well-defined.

Configurations exhibiting dynamically preferred metric degeneration are treated as non-admissible.

---

## 2. TIG2 Branch Compatibility

The vacuum geometry must remain compatible with the TIG2 branch structure

x^3 - x^2 + beta^3 = 0

including:

- admissible branch continuation,
- discriminant structure,
- saddle-node criticality,
- and near-critical scaling behavior.

Branch sectors violating structural consistency are excluded from the admissible class.

---

## 3. Bounded Geometric Sector

Admissible vacuum sectors should satisfy boundedness conditions for relevant geometric invariants.

Candidate admissibility conditions include bounded:

- Kretschmann scalar,
- Ricci scalar,
- Ricci contractions,
- and admissible higher-order curvature quantities.

The precise invariant class remains part of the TIG3 proof program.

---

## 4. Structural Stability

Admissible branch sectors should remain dynamically stable under sufficiently small admissible perturbations.

Possible stability indicators include:

- bounded relaxation behavior,
- controlled branch continuation,
- metastable near-critical sectors,
- and absence of dynamically preferred divergent modes.

Formal stability analysis remains open.

---

## 5. GR Limit Compatibility

Every admissible TIG3 sector must continuously recover standard GR behavior in the appropriate asymptotic regime.

In particular:

beta -> 0

must recover Schwarzschild-compatible behavior outside the regulated core sector.

This condition is treated as mandatory.

---

## 6. Near-Critical Admissibility

The near-critical regime

beta ≈ beta_c

requires special admissibility control due to enhanced structural sensitivity.

Potential phenomena include:

- branch merger behavior,
- amplified relaxation dynamics,
- near-critical scaling,
- and metastable geometric sectors.

The admissibility structure of this regime remains a central TIG3 research question.

---

# Excluded Interpretations

The TIG3 admissibility framework does not currently assume:

- complete singularity resolution,
- quantum-gravity completion,
- hidden dimensions,
- metaphysical structure,
- or observational verification.

The framework remains strictly structural and geometric.

---

# Structural Interpretation

The TIG3 admissibility problem investigates whether dynamically regulated high-curvature continuation may emerge naturally from structural branch consistency conditions.

The framework therefore studies:

- admissibility,
- bounded continuation,
- geometric regularization,
- and critical branch dynamics,

without claiming a complete gravitational replacement theory.
