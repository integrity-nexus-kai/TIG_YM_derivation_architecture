# Bounded Curvature Requirements

## Purpose

This document defines the preliminary bounded-curvature conditions imposed on admissible TIG3 vacuum geometries.

The objective is to investigate whether the TIG2 branch structure may admit dynamically regulated high-curvature continuation without requiring divergent geometric invariants.

The conditions collected here are structural admissibility targets and not yet formal theorems.

---

# Foundational Principle

Within classical singular collapse scenarios, curvature invariants may diverge as the collapse sector approaches geometric degeneration.

TIG3 instead investigates whether admissible branch geometries may dynamically remain within bounded curvature sectors.

The central structural question is therefore:

Can admissible TIG branch continuation remain geometrically finite throughout the regulated sector?

---

# Relevant Curvature Invariants

The bounded-curvature program primarily studies the behavior of:

- Kretschmann scalar,
- Ricci scalar,
- Ricci contractions,
- Weyl invariants,
- and admissible higher-order curvature quantities.

The precise invariant hierarchy remains part of the TIG3 proof program.

---

# Preliminary Boundedness Conditions

## 1. Finite Kretschmann Sector

Candidate admissibility condition:

sup |R_abcd R^abcd| < infinity

throughout the admissible regulated sector.

This condition represents the primary bounded-curvature target of the TIG3 program.

---

## 2. Finite Ricci Sector

Candidate admissibility conditions include bounded:

- Ricci scalar,
- Ricci contractions,
- and admissible stress-compatible geometric quantities.

The precise role of each invariant remains under investigation.

---

## 3. Controlled Metric Continuation

Bounded curvature alone is not sufficient.

The spacetime continuation must also remain:

- geometrically extendable,
- structurally admissible,
- and dynamically nondegenerate.

Admissibility therefore combines:

- invariant boundedness,
- metric regularity,
- and branch stability.

---

## 4. Near-Critical Curvature Amplification

The regime

beta ≈ beta_c

may exhibit strong curvature amplification without necessarily producing divergent geometric continuation.

Potential behaviors include:

- enhanced relaxation times,
- localized curvature concentration,
- metastable high-curvature sectors,
- and bounded near-critical amplification.

This regime is currently explored heuristically through TIG3 simulations.

---

# Relation to Singular Collapse

TIG3 does not currently claim a rigorous proof that singularities are impossible.

Instead, the framework investigates whether admissible branch geometries dynamically favor bounded continuation over divergent curvature sectors.

This distinction is essential.

The current program studies:

- structural admissibility,
- bounded geometric continuation,
- and dynamically regulated curvature sectors.

---

# Asymptotic Consistency

Any bounded-curvature TIG3 sector must remain compatible with the GR limit.

In particular:

beta -> 0

must recover Schwarzschild-compatible asymptotic behavior away from the regulated core region.

---

# Structural Interpretation

The bounded-curvature program treats geometric regularization as a structural consequence of admissible branch continuation rather than an externally imposed modification.

The central TIG3 hypothesis is therefore:

Admissible branch geometry may dynamically constrain the collapse sector into bounded high-curvature continuation.

This statement remains conjectural.
