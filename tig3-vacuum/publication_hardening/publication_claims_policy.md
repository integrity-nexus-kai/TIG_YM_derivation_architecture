# Publication Claims Policy

## Purpose

This document defines the canonical publication-claim policy for the TIG3 vacuum-sector framework.

Its purpose is:

- prevention of scientific overclaim,
- stabilization of publication language,
- reviewer compatibility,
- and long-term theorem integrity.

This document is part of the TIG3 publication-hardening layer.

---

# Core Principle

Every scientific statement within TIG3 must satisfy:

```text
claim <= mathematically justified result.
```

No interpretive statement may exceed the established theorem structure.

---

# Canonical Publication Philosophy

TIG3 follows the principle:

```text
structural rigor before interpretive ambition.
```

The framework prioritizes:

- explicit assumptions,
- mathematical transparency,
- and conditional theorem structure

over broad physical claims.

---

# Permitted Claim Categories

The TIG3 framework separates statements into:

| Category | Meaning |
|---|---|
| established | directly derived within current framework |
| conditional | dependent on stated assumptions |
| partial | structurally incomplete |
| conjectural | plausible but unproven |
| interpretive | physical interpretation only |
| observational | phenomenological outlook only |

Every major theorem statement should belong to one category.

The word:

```text

```

must always refer to:

```text

```

- microscopic quantum vacuum structure,
- metaphysical vacuum interpretation,
- or complete cosmological vacuum theory.

The word:


- conditional perturbative admissibility,
- bounded linear evolution,
- operator-theoretic stability.

Disallowed forms include:

- absolute stability,
- complete stability,
- or final stability proof.
