# TIG3 Research Architecture

## Purpose

This document defines the global research architecture of the TIG3 admissibility program.

The purpose of TIG3 is not to construct a speculative replacement theory of gravity, but to investigate whether the TIG2 branch structure imposes mathematically nontrivial admissibility conditions on vacuum geometry.

The framework is therefore organized as a structural-consistency program.

---

# Foundational Principle

TIG3 begins from the exact TIG2 branch equation

x^3 - x^2 + beta^3 = 0

together with:

- its discriminant structure,
- saddle-node criticality,
- near-critical scaling behavior,
- and admissible branch continuation.

The central TIG3 question is:

What class of vacuum geometries can consistently support this branch structure while remaining geometrically admissible and asymptotically GR-compatible?

---

# Core Program Structure

The TIG3 framework is divided into five interacting sectors:

1. Definitions
2. Constraints
3. Proof Program
4. Simulations
5. Open Questions

Each sector has a distinct mathematical role.

---

# 1. Definitions

The definitions sector establishes the minimal conceptual objects of the TIG3 framework.

Primary definitions include:

- admissible TIG vacuum geometry,
- structural control parameter,
- and regularized core region.

Purpose:

- eliminate conceptual ambiguity,
- define admissibility classes,
- and stabilize terminology.

The definitions sector acts as the foundational layer of the entire program.

---

# 2. Constraints

The constraints sector imposes structural consistency conditions on admissible TIG geometries.

Primary constraints include:

- bounded curvature requirements,
- GR limit consistency,
- and admissible vacuum continuation.

Purpose:

- restrict non-admissible geometries,
- preserve asymptotic consistency,
- and define structural admissibility.

The constraints sector acts as the geometric control layer of TIG3.

---

# 3. Proof Program

The proof-program sector organizes the logical derivation structure of TIG3.

Primary components include:

- lemma chains,
- theorem candidates,
- and admissibility logic.

Purpose:

- define mathematical dependency structure,
- separate proven and conjectural statements,
- and organize future rigorous derivations.

The proof program is currently structural and incomplete.

---

# 4. Simulations

The simulation sector provides heuristic structural visualization tools.

The simulations are not treated as formal proofs.

Their role is exploratory.

Primary targets include visualization of:

- near-critical branch dynamics,
- bounded high-curvature behavior,
- metastable continuation,
- and admissible branch stabilization.

Purpose:

- explore geometric intuition,
- identify possible dynamical regimes,
- and motivate future rigorous analysis.

---

# 5. Open Questions

The open-questions sector records unresolved mathematical problems.

Primary research targets include:

- covariant field structure,
- curvature control,
- nonlinear evolution,
- stability analysis,
- and admissible topology classes.

Purpose:

- prevent conceptual overreach,
- separate established structure from speculation,
- and define the active research frontier.

---

# Mathematical Strategy

The TIG3 program proceeds in the following order:

1. establish admissible branch structure,
2. define admissible vacuum geometry,
3. impose bounded-curvature conditions,
4. preserve GR consistency,
5. investigate near-critical dynamics,
6. study regulated continuation.

The framework therefore moves from:

- exact branch geometry,
- to admissibility,
- to stability,
- to bounded continuation.

This order is essential.

---

# Structural Interpretation

TIG3 interprets vacuum geometry as a constrained admissibility problem rather than an unrestricted singular continuation problem.

The framework investigates whether admissible branch geometry may dynamically favor:

- bounded continuation,
- regulated high-curvature sectors,
- and near-critical stabilization,

instead of divergent geometric collapse.

The framework remains conservative and geometric.

---

# Explicit Restrictions

TIG3 does not currently claim:

- singularity elimination,
- quantum-gravity completion,
- unification theory,
- observational confirmation,
- or replacement of general relativity.

The framework remains a structural admissibility program.

---

# Relation to GR

General relativity remains the asymptotic consistency anchor of TIG3.

In particular:

beta -> 0

must recover Schwarzschild-compatible behavior outside the regulated branch sector.

The GR limit is non-negotiable.

---

# Relation to Yang-Mills

Possible relations to Yang-Mills theory remain exploratory and explicitly separated from the formal TIG3 proof sector.

No Yang-Mills proof currently exists within TIG3.

---

# Current Status

TIG3 currently provides:

- a structural branch framework,
- admissibility conditions,
- bounded-curvature targets,
- preliminary proof architecture,
- and heuristic simulation structure.

The framework remains incomplete and programmatic.
