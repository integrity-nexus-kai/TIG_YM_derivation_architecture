# Simulation Assumptions

## Purpose

This document records the structural assumptions underlying the TIG3 visualization and simulation environment.

## Current Simulation Character

The current TIG3 simulations are heuristic visualization tools and not full numerical relativity solvers.

## Structural Assumptions

1. The TIG2 cubic branch structure governs the near-critical horizon sector.

2. The parameter beta acts as a structural control parameter.

3. High-curvature regions may dynamically regularize instead of forming divergent singular states.

4. Near-critical geometries may exhibit bounded relaxation modes.

5. Structural admissibility is treated as a geometric consistency requirement.

## Restrictions

The simulations currently do not provide:

- rigorous field equation derivations,
- numerical GR convergence proofs,
- full covariant evolution,
- observational validation,
- or quantum-gravity completion.
