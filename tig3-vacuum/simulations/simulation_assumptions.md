# Simulation Assumptions

## Purpose

This document records the current structural assumptions underlying the TIG3 visualization and simulation framework.

The simulations are not treated as formal proofs or numerical GR solvers.

Their purpose is exploratory and heuristic.

They are designed to visualize possible structural consequences of the TIG2 branch geometry within the TIG3 admissibility program.

---

# Foundational Principle

The TIG3 simulations investigate whether the TIG2 branch structure may admit:

- dynamically regulated high-curvature sectors,
- bounded near-critical continuation,
- metastable relaxation behavior,
- and admissible geometric stabilization.

The simulations therefore act as exploratory structural visualization tools.

---

# Core Structural Assumptions

## 1. TIG2 Branch Structure

The simulations assume the TIG2 cubic branch equation

x^3 - x^2 + beta^3 = 0

as the governing structural horizon relation.

The branch structure determines:

- admissible horizon sectors,
- critical transitions,
- discriminant behavior,
- and near-critical scaling.

---

## 2. Structural Control Parameter

The parameter

beta = r_c / (2M)

is treated as a continuous structural deformation parameter governing the branch geometry.

Within the simulations:

- increasing beta modifies branch behavior,
- near-critical beta enhances structural sensitivity,
- and branch deformation remains continuous outside the critical regime.

---

## 3. Regulated High-Curvature Sector

The simulations heuristically explore the possibility that admissible branch geometries may dynamically regulate the high-curvature regime.

Possible behaviors include:

- bounded curvature amplification,
- controlled branch continuation,
- metastable geometric response,
- and near-critical relaxation dynamics.

These behaviors are exploratory and not rigorously established.

---

## 4. Near-Critical Relaxation Dynamics

The simulations investigate whether the regime

beta ≈ beta_c

may exhibit:

- amplified relaxation times,
- quasi-periodic response,
- metastable branch behavior,
- or dynamically bounded oscillatory sectors.

The visualized dynamics are heuristic approximations and not derived from a full covariant evolution equation.

---

## 5. GR-Compatible Asymptotics

The simulations assume asymptotic compatibility with the GR limit.

In particular:

beta -> 0

is assumed to recover Schwarzschild-compatible behavior away from the regulated branch sector.

---

# Explicit Restrictions

The simulations do not currently provide:

- numerical relativity convergence,
- PDE stability proofs,
- full covariant evolution,
- observational validation,
- quantum-gravity completion,
- or formal singularity theorems.

They remain heuristic exploratory tools.

---

# Structural Interpretation

The TIG3 simulation program visualizes the possibility that admissible branch geometry may dynamically favor bounded continuation over unrestricted divergent collapse.

The simulations therefore explore:

- admissibility,
- bounded continuation,
- near-critical branch dynamics,
- and regulated high-curvature behavior,

within a conservative structural framework.

---

# Current Limitations

The current simulation framework remains incomplete.

Missing components include:

- rigorous evolution equations,
- stability operators,
- spectral analysis,
- and numerical convergence control.

These remain future research targets.
